# Defining and Enforcing “Definition of Done” for Power BI Stories

---

## 1. Introduction
- **Definition of Done (DoD)**: A **clear, shared checklist** of criteria that a Power BI user story must meet to be considered **complete** and ready for release. DoD ensures **consistency, quality, and transparency** across the team and stakeholders.
- **Why It Matters**: Without a DoD, teams risk delivering **incomplete, untested, or low-quality** Power BI solutions. A well-defined DoD aligns expectations, reduces rework, and improves stakeholder trust.

> [!NOTE]
> DoD is **not a one-size-fits-all** list—it should be **tailored** to your team’s maturity, project complexity, and stakeholder needs. It evolves as your Agile BI process matures.

---

## 2. Core Components of DoD for Power BI Stories

### 2.1 Functional Completeness
- **Requirements Met**: The story delivers all **accepted criteria** defined by the product owner/stakeholders.
- **Example**:
  - "The sales dashboard must show revenue by region and product line."

### 2.2 Data Accuracy and Validation
- **Data Quality**: Data is **validated**, **consistent**, and **accurate**.
  - **Example Checks**:
    - No nulls in critical fields (e.g., `Revenue`, `CustomerID`).
    - Data matches source systems (e.g., CRM, ERP).
- **Tools**: Use **Great Expectations**, **dbt tests**, or **Power BI data validation rules**.

### 2.3 Performance Standards
- **Load Times**: Dashboards/reports load within **acceptable thresholds** (e.g., <3 seconds).
- **Query Performance**: DAX measures and DirectQuery/SQL queries are optimized.
- **Example**:
  - "The dashboard must render in under 2 seconds with 50 concurrent users."

### 2.4 Security and Compliance
- **Access Controls**: **Row-level security (RLS)**, **data masking**, and **role-based access** are implemented.
- **Compliance**: Meets **GDPR**, **HIPAA**, or **internal policies** (e.g., PII protection).
- **Example**:
  - "RLS must restrict sales data to each user’s assigned region."

### 2.5 Testing and QA
- **Functional Testing**: All features work as intended (e.g., filters, drill-throughs, exports).
- **User Acceptance Testing (UAT)**: Stakeholders validate the solution meets their needs.
- **Example**:
  - "The marketing team must sign off on the customer segmentation report."

### 2.6 Documentation
- **Technical Documentation**: Data model, DAX measures, and ETL logic are documented (e.g., in **Tabular Editor** or **Confluence**).
- **User Documentation**: Guides or **Loom videos** for end-users.
- **Example**:
  - "Document the data lineage for the sales dataset in Confluence."

### 2.7 Deployment and Release
- **Deployment**: Successfully deployed to the **target environment** (Dev/Test/Prod).
- **Release Notes**: Clear notes on **changes, known issues, and usage instructions**.
- **Example**:
  - "Deploy the sales dashboard to the Prod workspace and notify users via Teams."

### 2.8 Stakeholder Sign-Off
- **Approval**: Stakeholders confirm the story meets their expectations.
- **Example**:
  - "The product owner must approve the final dashboard before release."

## 3. Example Definition of Done for Power BI Stories

### **Basic DoD (Minimum Viable)**
| Criteria                          | Details                                                                 |
|-----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Functional Completeness**       | All acceptance criteria are met.                                      |
| **Data Accuracy**                 | Data is validated for nulls and outliers.                             |
| **Performance**                   | Dashboard loads in <5 seconds.                                         |
| **Security**                     | RLS is applied for sensitive data.                                    |
| **Testing**                       | Functional testing completed by the BI team.                          |
| **Deployment**                   | Deployed to the Test environment.                                       |
| **Stakeholder Sign-Off**          | Product owner approves the story.                                      |

---

### **Advanced DoD (Mature Teams)**
| Criteria                          | Details                                                                 |
|-----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Functional Completeness**       | All acceptance criteria are met and demoed to stakeholders.          |
| **Data Accuracy**                 | Data validated with **Great Expectations** or **dbt tests**.          |
| **Performance**                   | Dashboard loads in <2 seconds with 100 concurrent users.              |
| **Security**                     | RLS, data masking, and **Azure AD integration** are implemented.      |
| **Testing**                       | Functional, UAT, and **performance testing** completed.               |
| **Documentation**                 | Data model, DAX measures, and user guide documented in Confluence.     |
| **Deployment**                   | Deployed to Prod with **rollback plan** and **release notes**.       |
| **Stakeholder Sign-Off**          | Product owner and **end-users** approve the story.                   |
| **Monitoring**                   | **Power BI Admin Portal** alerts set up for errors/performance issues. |

## 4. Tailoring DoD to Your Team

### 4.1 Start Small, Iterate
- Begin with a **basic DoD** and add criteria as the team matures.
- **Example**:
  - **Sprint 1**: Focus on functional completeness and basic testing.
  - **Sprint 5**: Add performance thresholds and automated validation.

### 4.2 Align with Stakeholders
- Collaborate with stakeholders to define **what "done" means for them**.
- **Example**:
  - "For financial reports, stakeholders require **audit trails** and **sign-off from Finance**."

### 4.3 Differentiate by Story Type
- **Adapt DoD** based on the complexity and risk of the story:
  - **Low Risk** (e.g., minor dashboard tweaks): Basic DoD.
  - **High Risk** (e.g., financial reports): Advanced DoD with audits.

| Story Type               | DoD Criteria to Emphasize                          |
|--------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| **Prototype**            | Functional completeness, stakeholder sign-off.    |
| **Production Report**    | Data accuracy, performance, security, UAT.        |
| **ETL Pipeline**         | Data validation, performance, documentation.      |
| **Ad-Hoc Analysis**      | Functional completeness, quick stakeholder review. |

---

## 5. Enforcing the Definition of Done

### 5.1 Make DoD Visible
- **Post in Workspaces**: Display the DoD in **Teams channels**, **Confluence**, or **Jira/Azure DevOps**.
- **Example**:
  - Add the DoD as a **checklist** in every Power BI user story.

### 5.2 Use DoD in Sprint Planning
- **Estimate with DoD in Mind**: Ensure stories are sized to include all DoD criteria.
- **Example**:
  - "This story requires UAT, so let’s add 2 days for testing."

### 5.3 Automate Checks
- **CI/CD Pipelines**: Use **Azure DevOps** or **GitHub Actions** to automate validation (e.g., dbt tests, Power BI deployment checks).
- **Example**:
  - Block deployments if **data quality tests** fail.

### 5.4 Retrospective Reviews
- **Inspect and Adapt**: Review DoD compliance in **sprint retrospectives**.
  - **Example Questions**:
    - "Did we meet the DoD for all stories this sprint?"
    - "Should we add/remove any DoD criteria?"

### 5.5 Train the Team
- **Onboarding**: Ensure all team members understand the DoD and its importance.
- **Example**:
  - Hold a **workshop** on how to apply the DoD to Power BI stories.

## 6. Common Pitfalls and Mitigations

| Pitfall                          | Mitigation                                                                 |
|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **DoD is too rigid**             | Tailor DoD to story type and risk. Start with a basic DoD and iterate.     |
| **DoD is ignored**               | Enforce DoD in **sprint planning** and **retrospectives**.                |
| **Stakeholders don’t understand DoD** | Educate stakeholders on **why DoD matters** and **their role** in sign-off. |
| **DoD slows down delivery**      | Automate checks (e.g., dbt tests) and focus on **high-impact criteria**. |
| **Inconsistent application**     | Use **checklists** in Jira/Azure DevOps and **peer reviews**.           |

## 8. Flashcard-Style Q&A

**Q: What is the "Definition of Done" (DoD) in Agile BI?**
**A:** A shared checklist of criteria a Power BI user story must meet to be considered complete and ready for release.

**Q: Why is DoD important for Power BI stories?**
**A:** It ensures **consistency, quality, and transparency**, reducing rework and aligning stakeholder expectations.

**Q: What are the core components of DoD for Power BI?**
**A:** Functional completeness, data accuracy, performance, security, testing, documentation, deployment, and stakeholder sign-off.

**Q: How can you tailor DoD to different story types?**
**A:** Adapt criteria based on **risk and complexity** (e.g., prototypes vs. financial reports).

**Q: What’s a good way to enforce DoD?**
**A:** Use **checklists in Jira/Azure DevOps**, automate checks with **CI/CD pipelines**, and review compliance in **sprint retrospectives**.

**Q: How can you automate DoD checks?**
**A:** Use tools like **Great Expectations** (data validation), **dbt** (testing), and **Power BI REST API** (deployment checks).

**Q: What should you do if stakeholders don’t understand DoD?**
**A:** Educate them on **why it matters** and **their role** in sign-off. Provide examples of how DoD prevents issues.

**Q: How can you start with DoD if your team is new to Agile BI?**
**A:** Begin with a **basic DoD** (e.g., functional completeness, testing, stakeholder sign-off) and iterate as the team matures.

## 9. Real-World Example: Financial Reporting Team

**Scenario**: A financial services team builds **regulatory reports** in Power BI. Accuracy and compliance are critical, but stakeholders demand faster delivery.

**DoD Approach**:
1. **Define DoD**:
   - **Data Accuracy**: Validated against source systems with **audit trails**.
   - **Security**: RLS and **data masking** for sensitive fields.
   - **Compliance**: Sign-off from **Finance and Legal**.
   - **Performance**: Reports load in <5 seconds.
   - **Testing**: UAT by Finance team.

2. **Enforce DoD**:
   - Use **Azure DevOps** to block deployments if **data validation fails**.
   - Hold **weekly reviews** with Finance to sign off on reports.

3. **Tailor for Speed**:
   - For **exploratory analysis**, use a **lighter DoD** (e.g., functional completeness + stakeholder review).
   - For **regulatory reports**, enforce the **full DoD**.

**Outcome**:
- **Regulatory reports** are **accurate and compliant**.
- **Exploratory analysis** is delivered **faster** without sacrificing quality.
